Big Island

The Big Island is the largest and most geologically active island in Hawaii, with everything from volcanoes to snowy mountains.

  • Mauna Kea gets snow in the winter
  • It has 10 of the world's 14 climate zones
  • You can visit an active lava flow (when safe)
  • Home to the world's tallest mountain (Mauna Kea, measured from base to peak)

Maui

Maui is famous for its luxury resorts, scenic drives, and natural beauty. It's perfect for adventure and relaxation.

  • The Road to Hāna has over 600 curves and 50+ bridges
  • You can hike into a volcanic crater at Haleakala
  • Lahaina town was once the capital of the Hawaiian Kingdom
  • Popular for snorkeling at Molokini Crater

O'ahu

O'ahu is the cultural and political heart of Hawaii, where the past and present mix in a lively island setting.

  • Diamond Head is an extinct volcanic crater you can hike
  • Iolani Palace is the only royal palace in the U.S.
  • There are over 125 beaches around the island
  • “O'ahu” means “The Gathering Place”

Kaua'i

Kaua'i is known for its lush rainforests and dramatic cliffs, making it a favorite for outdoor lovers and filmmakers.

  • Parts of Jurassic Park and Avatar were filmed here
  • It's over 5 million years old — the oldest Hawaiian island
  • Mount Wai'ale'ale is one of the wettest spots on Earth
  • You can only reach some areas by boat, helicopter, or foot
